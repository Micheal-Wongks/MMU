German food is rich, hearty and diverse. It's comfort eating with high-quality, often locally sourced ingredients. The cuisine of Germany has been shaped not only by the country's agricultural traditions but by the many immigrants that have made the country home over the centuries. It's definitely more than a mere mix of beer, sauerkraut and sausage.

Today Germans appreciate well-prepared, well-served meals as much as they do a quick bite on the go. This is a country of food markets, beer gardens, wine festivals, food museums and high-end restaurants. We have made a list of some dishes tourists need to try when they travel to Germany.

1. Sausages

There is no Germany without sausages. There are countless cured, smoked and other varieties available across wurst-loving Germany, so, for this list we will focus on some of the best German street food: bratwurst, or fried sausages.There are more than 40 varieties of German bratwurst. Fried on a barbecue or in the pan, and then served in a white bread roll with mustard on the go, or with potato salad as the perfect accompaniment for German beer.

2. Spätzle

Germany’s version of pasta, spätzle, is made from a simple yet exquisite combination of eggs, flour, salt and a splash of sparkling water. Originating from Baden-Württemberg, this tasty dish is traditionally served as an accompaniment to meat dishes or used to hearten up soups. This simple side dish can be transformed into something mighty with the simple addition of Emmental or Swiss cheese.

3. Schnitzel

You’d be forgiven for thinking schnitzel is Austrian rather than German, but what you might not know is that this breaded meat dish actually originated in Italy. While the Austrian ‘Wiener Schnitzel’ is by law only made with veal, the German version is made with tenderised pork or turkey, and is a staple of most traditional German restaurants.

4. Sauerkraut

Claimed by both France and Germany, the flammkuchen hails from the Alsace region, which in the past has been a dispute between the two countries. A sort of German pizza, flammkuchen is a thinly-rolled, rectangular shaped bread dough traditionally topped with crème fraîche, thinly sliced onions and lardons. Finished by being baked in an oven, this delightful delicacy packs a comforting yet delicious salty punch that’ll leave you wanting more.

5. Beer

This isn’t technically a foodie dish, but we couldn’t write a food guide to Germany without mention of the nation’s favourite beverage. Beer, or bier as the Germans call it, even has its own festival – Oktoberfest runs each September and celebrates all things beer for around two weeks. As the birthplace of lager, most German beers are held to the Reinheitsgebot (the German Beer Purity Law) that originated in the sixteenth century.

Restaurants

1. Marjellchen

If you are looking for one of the fine dining restaurants in Berlin, you should head to Marjellchen. Considered to be one of the best restaurants in Berlin, Marjellchen offers traditional German food. While the service is impeccable, the restaurant serves delicious dishes from East Prussia and offers beautiful views of the surrounding.

2. Restaurant Atelier Gourmet

Restaurant Atelier Gourmet is a popular eatery that serves French cuisine, and a few European dishes as well. One of the most popular eateries among locals and travellers, this cosy restaurant gets very busy during dinner hours. Because it is so popular and usually has a long line of people waiting to get in, guests are expected to leave as soon as they finish their meal. Nevertheless, Restaurant Atelier Gourmet is among the must-visit restaurants in Munich Germany.

  • Must Try: 4 course menu, Beef, Wine
  • Timings: 6 PM to 1AM, Sunday Closed
  • Location: Rablstraße 37, 81669 München, Germany
  • Phone: +49 89 487220

3. Ratskeller Munchen

If you want to get a taste of authentic local cuisine, visit the one and only Ratskeller. This is one of the best German restaurants for trying dishes like lamb shank, fish cakes, pancakes etc. and also a nice place for a drink with friends. The restaurant has an old-world charm that one will surely love along with which, one can always expect great food, drinks, and excellent service at this restaurant.

  • Must Try: Potato Pancakes, Pretzels, Sauerbraten
  • Timings: 10 AM to 12 AM
  • Location: Marienplatz 8 | 80331 Munich, 80331 Munich, Bavaria, Germany
  • Phone: +49 89 2199890

4. Ebbelwoi Unser

Ebbelwoi Unser Restaurant is among the good restaurants in Germany that is known for providing lip-smacking German food to its guests. From the beautiful ambience to an ideal location, and from the courteous staff to being pocket-friendly, this restaurant is a must try for all travelers when in Germany!

  • Must Try: Potato Sausage, Apple Wine, Schnitzel
  • Timings: 5 PM to 1 AM
  • Location: Abtsgäßchen 8 | Eingang Kleine Brückenstraße 15, 60594 Fr
  • Phone: +49 1520 4302659

5. Steinheil 16

When it comes to the best restaurants in Germany, there is no place quite like Steinheil 16. This is a simple, no frills eatery that serves some of the most delicious local dishes in the city. It is affordable and has a welcoming, helpful staff. If you have a liking for authentic German food or pure vegetarian cuisine during your stay in Munich, Steinheil 16 is the place to be.

  • Must Try: Potatoes, Schnitzel
  • Timings: 10 AM to 1 AM
  • Location: Steinheilstr. 16, 80333 Munich, Bavaria, Germany
  • Phone: +49 89 527488
