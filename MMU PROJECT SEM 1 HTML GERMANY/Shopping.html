5. German Chocolate

German chocolate ranks with Swiss and Belgian chocolate as the finest in the world. In fact, Germany is a nation of chocoholics, with the second highest annual chocolate consumption in the world (just slightly behind Switzerland). Cologne is often regarded as the chocolate capital of Germany and is home to the Stollwerck Chocolate Company and the legendary Chocolate Museum.

~Best Places For Shopping In Germany

1. Schildergasse, Cologne

Considered to be the busiest shopping street in Europe, Cologne's Schildergasse has a long history that dates back to ancient Roman times.Today it is amongst the best places for shopping in Germany with major department stores and fashion outlets like Galeria Kaufhof, Zara, H&M, etc.The centre of attraction here is the Weltstadthaus, a huge glass structure resembling a whale that houses a lovely clothes store.

2. Zeil, Frankfurt

Also known as the Fifth Avenue of Germany, this shopping street in Frankfurt is a delight for the discerning shopper. The area between Konstablerwache and Hauptwache is lined with the best international brand stores and chic boutiques offering the best in fashion and high living. While you're exploring this fantastic promenade, do not miss visiting the 10-storey Zeilgalerie with its breathtaking architecture and a wonderful viewing platform offering the best views of Frankfurt. The adjoining Goethestrasse is the best place to shop for designer jewelry

3. Ku'damm, Berlin

Kurfurstendamm, simply known as Ku'damm is Berlin's most popular shopping street and the best place to hang out and spend an enjoyable evening. Get you dose of latest fashion trends and pick some classy stuff from Armani, Chanel, YSL and chic boutique stores. The cool new shopping centre, Bikini Berlin has lots of trendy stores and pop-up shops, while Berlin's largest department store KaDeWe offers a world of beauty, garments, fashion, and accessories spread over 6 storeys.

4. Konigsallee & Schadowstrasse, Dusseldorf

In the heart of Dusseldorf this upscale promenade with a tree-lined river and numerous boutiques and designer stores is an elegant place for some high-end shopping in Germany. Called Ko by the locals, connoisseurs can shop to their heart's content at the luxury stores and shopping malls along this street. Another fantastic shopping destination, Dusseldorf's longest and most popular shopping street Schadowstrasse has more than 200 shops that offer everything that you could wish for without burning a hole in your pocket.

5. Ingolstadt Village Designer Outlet

For a slightly different shopping experience, head to this shopping village in Germany that's home to world-class designer brands like Prada, Coach, Gucci, and Versace. Just an hour away from Munich, the place is good for tourists as well as shopaholics. You can grab some good discounts during the yearly promotional sales on branded products. The Shopping Express shuttle is a great way to reach this designer outlet, or you catch a bus which is a more reasonable option. With restaurants and cafes, the Ingolstadt village offers a relaxed shopping experience away from the bustle of the city.
